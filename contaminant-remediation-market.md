<p><p>The contaminant remediation market is divided into soil and groundwater segments. Soil remediation focuses on the treatment of contaminated land, utilizing techniques such as bioremediation, thermal desorption, and chemical treatment to restore soil quality. Groundwater remediation addresses pollutants in underground water sources, employing methods like pump and treat, in-situ chemical oxidation, and permeable reactive barriers to remove contaminants. Both markets aim to ensure environmental safety, promote sustainable land use, and safeguard public health by restoring affected natural resources.</p></p>
